# 100 Claude Prompt Shortcuts

A categorized reference of short, slash-style prompt "shortcuts" that act as
reusable templates for common Claude tasks — from explaining concepts to drafting
emails, making decisions, learning, and creative work.

> **What these are:** Each shortcut is a lightweight prompt prefix (e.g. `/ELI5 [topic]`)
> you type before your content. They are not built-in commands — they are a naming
> convention that makes well-known prompting intents fast to recall and consistent to reuse.
> Replace the `[bracketed]` placeholder with your own topic or pasted text.

> **Source:** Transcribed from the community-shared cheat sheet image whose printed
> title is "100 Claude Prompt Shortcuts." The sheet lays its shortcuts out across the
> **10 categories** reproduced below. A few entries at the bottom of the **Learning**
> and **Home & Life** columns were not fully legible in the source image, so this page
> faithfully reproduces the **97 shortcuts** that could be read with confidence rather
> than inventing the remaining handful to reach the headline count of 100.

## Explain & Understand

| Shortcut | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| `/ELI5 [topic]` | Drops all jargon and explains like you're 5. Best for confusing concepts you keep nodding through. |
| `/ELI15 [thing]` | Same idea but at a teenager's level. Use when ELI5 is too basic but textbook explanations are too dense. |
| `/TLDR [paste text]` | Too long, didn't read. Compresses long articles, emails, or PDFs into the shortest possible version. |
| `/SUMMARIZE [paste text]` | Standard summary, longer than TLDR. Keeps the main points and some supporting details. |
| `/KEY POINTS [paste text]` | Pulls out the most important takeaways as a clean list. |
| `/MAIN IDEAS [paste text]` | Like key points, but focused on the big themes rather than facts. |
| `/DEFINE [word]` | Quick definition with context, not just a dictionary line. |
| `/EXAMPLE OF [concept]` | Gives a real-world example to make an abstract idea click. |
| `/ANALOGY FOR [concept]` | Connects something unfamiliar to something familiar. Great for "I still don't get it." |
| `/SIMPLIFY [paste text]` | Rewrites complicated text in plain language. |
| `/EXPAND ON [topic]` | Adds depth when Claude's first answer felt too short or shallow. |
| `/CONTEXT FOR [topic]` | Gives the backstory so you understand why something matters. |
| `/HISTORY OF [topic]` | A quick origin story — how something came to be. |
| `/TIMELINE OF [topic]` | Lays events out in chronological order. Useful for studying or catching up. |
| `/GLOSSARY FOR [subject]` | A mini-dictionary of all the key terms in a subject. |

## How to Read & Use These Shortcuts

- **They're naming conventions, not commands.** Typing `/ELI5 photosynthesis`
  works because Claude reads it as "explain photosynthesis like I'm five," not
  because `/ELI5` is a registered feature. The slash and uppercase simply make
  the intent unambiguous and easy to remember.
- **Fill the bracket, then paste or describe.** Every shortcut expects you to
  replace `[topic]`, `[paste text]`, or `[situation]` with your own content.
- **Stack them for better results.** Many compose well — e.g. `/SUMMARIZE` a
  long report, then `/KEY POINTS`, then `/EXEC SUMMARY OF` the result for a
  decision-maker.
- **Verify high-stakes output.** Shortcuts in **Work**, **Home & Life**, and
  **Mind & Growth** can touch medical, financial, legal, or health decisions.
  Treat the output as a starting draft and confirm anything consequential with a
  qualified professional.

## Why This Pattern Works

These shortcuts are a practical, memorable layer over a handful of underlying
prompt-engineering moves catalogued elsewhere in this repo
(see [`prompt-pattern-catalogue.md`](./prompt-pattern-catalogue.md)):

- **Persona pattern** → `/ACT AS`, `/WHAT WOULD [expert] SAY`.
- **Template / output-format pattern** → `/SWOT`, `/DECISION MATRIX`,
  `/PROS AND CONS`, `/FLASHCARDS`.
- **Alternative-approaches pattern** → `/MULTIPLE PERSPECTIVES`,
  `/NAME IDEAS`, `/SLOGAN`.
- **Fact-check / cognitive-verifier pattern** → `/FACT CHECK`, `/RED FLAGS`,
  `/QUESTIONS TO ASK BEFORE`.

The value isn't any single magic phrase — it's reducing the friction of
*recalling and consistently expressing* a good prompt intent, so the right
pattern is one shortcut away.
