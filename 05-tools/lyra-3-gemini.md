# Lyria 3 in Gemini: 6 Prompting Tips Tutorial

## Intent
- Create stronger 30-second music generations with Lyria 3 in the Gemini app.
- Move from simple prompts to richer, more controlled outputs.
- Share finished tracks quickly once you get a result you like.

## Prerequisites
- Access to Gemini app with **Create music** (Lyria 3).
- Optional: an image or short video you can upload as inspiration.

## Quick start
1. Open Gemini.
2. Select **Create music**.
3. Enter a text prompt (or add an image/video).
4. Iterate your prompt using the six tips below.
5. Download and share your result as MP3/MP4.

---

## 1) Begin with text
Start with a plain-language idea that includes topic + mood + style.

**Template**
```text
Create a [genre/style] track about [topic].
Mood: [emotion].
Vocals: [type/accent/tone] or instrumental only.
```

**Examples**
```text
Create an Afrobeats track about my favorite meal: rice, plantains, and beans.
Mood: warm and nostalgic.
Vocals: West African-sounding singer.
```

```text
Create a 90s skate-punk rock track telling my roommate Ryan to wash the dishes.
Mood: high energy.
Drums: fast and aggressive.
```

---

## 2) Upload an image or video
Use visuals to anchor tone, atmosphere, and pacing.

**What works well**
- Travel and holiday photos.
- Pet photos.
- Artwork/illustrations.
- Short clips with clear mood and movement.

**Prompt add-on after upload**
```text
Use this image/video as the creative direction.
Match the mood, colors, and energy.
```

---

## 3) Define the genre and era
Genre + time period gives Lyria a strong stylistic frame.

**Starter patterns**
```text
Genre: 90s hip-hop
Genre: 2000s pop
Genre blend: K-pop with Motown influence
Genre blend: funk with classical violin textures
```

**Tip**
If results feel generic, specify both era and subgenre (for example, “late-90s East Coast hip-hop” instead of just “hip-hop”).

---

## 4) Add details: instruments, song dynamics, vocals
This is where most quality gains happen.

**Instrument control**
- Name must-have instruments (e.g., sax, trumpet, 80s synth, nylon guitar).
- If you do not specify instruments, Lyria chooses automatically.

**Song dynamics**
- Tell Lyria how the track should evolve: intro -> verse -> chorus -> bridge -> outro.
- Describe intensity shifts (quiet opening, explosive chorus, stripped breakdown).

**Vocal direction**
- Vocal type: male/female/choir.
- Vocal range: baritone/soprano.
- Texture descriptors: gravelly, breathy, soulful, rich.
- Harmony ideas: call-and-response, layered harmonies, quieter ending.

**Example**
```text
Create a 1950s jazz-inspired track with sax and trumpet, plus subtle 80s synth pads.
Structure: soft piano intro, energetic brass chorus, short instrumental bridge, gentle outro.
Vocals: female alto, soulful and warm, with light harmonies in the final chorus.
```

---

## 5) Write your own lyrics — or ask Lyria to handle it
You can provide lyrics directly or let Lyria generate them from a theme.

**A) Provide your own lyrics**
Use the `Lyrics:` label and keep lines short (tracks are 30 seconds).

```text
Create a bright pop track about friendship.
Lyrics: We made it through the rain (through the rain),
Now we're dancing in the sun (in the sun).
```

**B) Ask Lyria to write lyrics**
Give a clear theme and tone.

```text
Create a hopeful R&B track about starting over after failure.
Please generate concise lyrics that fit a 30-second format.
```

---

## 6) Share your tracks
Once satisfied, export and share.

- Download as **MP3** or **MP4**.
- Post to social platforms or send in chats.
- Keep prompt versions so you can reproduce or remix favorites.

**Simple naming convention**
```text
[genre]-[era]-[theme]-v1
Example: funk-90s-dishwashing-joke-v3
```

---

## Copy-paste master prompt
```text
Create a 30-second track.
Genre/era: [insert].
Theme: [insert].
Mood: [insert].
Instruments: [insert].
Dynamics: [intro -> verse -> chorus -> outro instructions].
Vocals: [type, range, texture, harmony instructions] OR instrumental only.
Lyrics: [insert your lines] OR generate lyrics about [theme].
```
