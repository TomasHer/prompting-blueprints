# Open Models for Agentic AI: Why Openness Accelerates Innovation

## The core idea
> “When AI is open, it proliferates everywhere.” — Jensen Huang

That one sentence captures the strategic shift behind today’s open model movement. Open models lower the barriers to experimentation, enable local and sovereign deployments, and let builders adapt models to their own data, policies, and workflows. For AI agents—systems that must reason, plan, and act across tools—openness expands what can be built and who can build it.

## Why open models matter for agentic systems
Open models create leverage across the full agent lifecycle:

- **Faster iteration**: Teams can fine-tune, evaluate, and deploy models without waiting on closed vendor roadmaps.
- **Broader adoption**: Startups, researchers, students, and public-sector teams can build with fewer licensing constraints.
- **Infrastructure flexibility**: Open weights let you run agents on-prem, at the edge, or in regions with strict data residency requirements.
- **Transparency and trust**: Auditing, benchmarking, and red-teaming are more feasible when the model and its behavior are accessible.

These dynamics are why open models are fueling the AI revolution and why they matter for agentic use cases that span tooling, orchestration, and safety.

## Practical ways to use open models in agent stacks
If you’re building or upgrading an AI agent, open models like Nemotron unlock deployment patterns that are hard to achieve with closed-only systems:

1. **Local or sovereign deployments**: Run agents close to regulated data and audit the full pipeline.
2. **Custom tool policies**: Align behavior to internal policy by fine-tuning or instruction tuning.
3. **Hybrid routing**: Use open models for low-latency, high-volume steps and call premium models only when needed.
4. **Edge inference**: Support offline or constrained environments while keeping agent behavior consistent.

## A simple checklist for evaluating open models
When comparing open model options for agentic AI, start with a short, practical checklist:

- **Task alignment**: Can the model follow tool instructions and structured outputs consistently?
- **Cost/latency profile**: Does it meet your real-time budget for multi-step workflows?
- **Modality coverage**: Does it support the inputs your agents need (text, image, structured data)?
- **Deployment constraints**: Can it run where your data lives—cloud, on-prem, or edge?
- **Governance**: Are safety filters, evaluation harnesses, and audit logs feasible in your stack?

## Key takeaway
Open models aren’t just cheaper or more accessible—they are strategic infrastructure for agentic AI. NVIDIA Nemotron highlights this direction: open, efficient, and multimodal foundations that let teams build trustworthy, scalable agents across industries and regions.
