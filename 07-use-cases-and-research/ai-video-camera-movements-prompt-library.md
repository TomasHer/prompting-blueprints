# AI Video Camera Movements Prompt Library (42 Moves)

## Intent
Use this library to quickly compose high-quality AI video prompts with precise camera movement language. It is designed for text-to-video tools (for example Veo, Sora-style workflows, Kling, Runway, Pika, and similar generators).

## Use when
- You want more cinematic motion control in AI video outputs.
- You need copy-ready movement lines you can paste into a larger shot prompt.
- You want a standardized list of the 42 camera movement prompts in one place.

## Output format
Use this minimal structure in your prompting workflow:

```markdown
Subject + scene
Camera movement line
Visual style + lens + lighting
Timing + speed + mood
Negative constraints
```

## The 42 Camera Movements for AI Video Prompts

1. **Slow dolly in, camera moves slowly forward toward the subject.**
   - Example: `Slow dolly in, camera moves slowly forward toward the subject, a violinist under warm stage lights, shallow depth of field, cinematic realism.`
2. **Slow dolly out, camera moves slowly backward away from the subject.**
   - Example: `Slow dolly out, camera moves slowly backward away from the subject, revealing a lone astronaut in a vast crater at blue hour.`
3. **Fast dolly in, camera quickly pushes forward toward the subject.**
   - Example: `Fast dolly in, camera quickly pushes forward toward the subject as the detective notices a hidden clue on the table.`
4. **Fast dolly out, camera quickly pulls backward away from the subject.**
   - Example: `Fast dolly out, camera quickly pulls backward away from the subject to reveal a crowded neon market at night.`
5. **Truck left, camera moves horizontally to the left.**
   - Example: `Truck left, camera moves horizontally to the left alongside a runner on a rainy city street.`
6. **Truck right, camera moves horizontally to the right.**
   - Example: `Truck right, camera moves horizontally to the right, tracking a chef plating food in a modern kitchen.`
7. **Pedestal up, camera rises vertically upward.**
   - Example: `Pedestal up, camera rises vertically upward from a chessboard to the focused face of the player.`
8. **Pedestal down, camera lowers vertically downward.**
   - Example: `Pedestal down, camera lowers vertically downward from skyline level to a taxi waiting at the curb.`
9. **Pan left, camera rotates left on a fixed axis.**
   - Example: `Pan left, camera rotates left on a fixed axis across a gallery wall of abstract paintings.`
10. **Pan right, camera rotates right on a fixed axis.**
    - Example: `Pan right, camera rotates right on a fixed axis following birds taking off from a pier.`
11. **Tilt up, camera tilts upward on a fixed axis.**
    - Example: `Tilt up, camera tilts upward on a fixed axis from street level to towering glass skyscrapers.`
12. **Tilt down, camera tilts downward on a fixed axis.**
    - Example: `Tilt down, camera tilts downward on a fixed axis from storm clouds to waves hitting black rocks.`
13. **Roll clockwise, camera rotates clockwise along the lens axis.**
    - Example: `Roll clockwise, camera rotates clockwise along the lens axis during a surreal dream sequence in a hallway.`
14. **Roll counterclockwise, camera rotates counterclockwise along the lens axis.**
    - Example: `Roll counterclockwise, camera rotates counterclockwise along the lens axis as paper pages swirl in the air.`
15. **Arc left, camera curves around the subject to the left.**
    - Example: `Arc left, camera curves around the subject to the left around a boxer preparing in the ring.`
16. **Arc right, camera curves around the subject to the right.**
    - Example: `Arc right, camera curves around the subject to the right around a bride standing in a candlelit hall.`
17. **Orbit 360, camera circles fully around the subject.**
    - Example: `Orbit 360, camera circles fully around the subject, a dancer frozen mid-pose in drifting smoke.`
18. **Push-in orbit, camera moves in while circling the subject.**
    - Example: `Push-in orbit, camera moves in while circling the subject, a wizard casting light particles in a dark forest.`
19. **Pull-out reveal, camera moves out to reveal wider context.**
    - Example: `Pull-out reveal, camera moves out to reveal wider context: the tiny cabin sits alone on a snowy mountain.`
20. **Slow zoom in, lens zooms in gradually toward the subject.**
    - Example: `Slow zoom in, lens zooms in gradually toward the subject, a child reading by window light, soft film grain.`
21. **Slow zoom out, lens zooms out gradually from the subject.**
    - Example: `Slow zoom out, lens zooms out gradually from the subject to reveal an ancient library interior.`
22. **Crash zoom in, lens zooms in rapidly for dramatic emphasis.**
    - Example: `Crash zoom in, lens zooms in rapidly for dramatic emphasis on the hero's eyes before the duel.`
23. **Crash zoom out, lens zooms out rapidly for sudden reveal.**
    - Example: `Crash zoom out, lens zooms out rapidly for sudden reveal of a giant creature behind the hikers.`
24. **Rack focus near-to-far, focus shifts from foreground to background.**
    - Example: `Rack focus near-to-far, focus shifts from a coffee cup in foreground to a commuter train arriving outside.`
25. **Rack focus far-to-near, focus shifts from background to foreground.**
    - Example: `Rack focus far-to-near, focus shifts from fireworks in the distance to a smiling couple in foreground.`
26. **Follow shot, camera tracks behind the moving subject.**
    - Example: `Follow shot, camera tracks behind the moving subject, a cyclist riding through an autumn park path.`
27. **Lead shot, camera tracks backward in front of the moving subject.**
    - Example: `Lead shot, camera tracks backward in front of the moving subject, a child running through sprinklers.`
28. **Side tracking shot, camera tracks parallel to the subject.**
    - Example: `Side tracking shot, camera tracks parallel to the subject, a horse galloping across open grassland.`
29. **Top-down drop, camera descends from overhead toward the subject.**
    - Example: `Top-down drop, camera descends from overhead toward the subject, a sushi platter on a wooden table.`
30. **Top-down rise, camera ascends from the subject to an overhead view.**
    - Example: `Top-down rise, camera ascends from the subject to an overhead view of dancers forming geometric patterns.`
31. **Drone rise, camera lifts high into the sky.**
    - Example: `Drone rise, camera lifts high into the sky above a lighthouse on a stormy coastline.`
32. **Drone dive, camera dives downward from high altitude.**
    - Example: `Drone dive, camera dives downward from high altitude toward surfers waiting beyond the break.`
33. **Fly-through, camera moves continuously through the environment.**
    - Example: `Fly-through, camera moves continuously through the environment: from street, through café window, to barista close-up.`
34. **Whip pan left, camera snaps quickly to the left.**
    - Example: `Whip pan left, camera snaps quickly to the left from one musician to another during a live jam session.`
35. **Whip pan right, camera snaps quickly to the right.**
    - Example: `Whip pan right, camera snaps quickly to the right to reveal a speeding motorcycle entering frame.`
36. **Handheld shake, camera has subtle natural handheld motion.**
    - Example: `Handheld shake, camera has subtle natural handheld motion while documenting a protest march at dusk.`
37. **Steadicam glide, camera moves smoothly with stabilized motion.**
    - Example: `Steadicam glide, camera moves smoothly with stabilized motion through a hotel corridor in one take.`
38. **Locked-off shot, camera remains static with no movement.**
    - Example: `Locked-off shot, camera remains static with no movement as snow falls outside a cabin window.`
39. **Parallax slide, camera slides sideways with layered depth separation.**
    - Example: `Parallax slide, camera slides sideways with layered depth separation through bamboo in a misty garden.`
40. **Spiral in, camera circles while moving inward toward the subject.**
    - Example: `Spiral in, camera circles while moving inward toward the subject, a saxophonist in moody blue light.`
41. **Spiral out, camera circles while moving outward from the subject.**
    - Example: `Spiral out, camera circles while moving outward from the subject, revealing a ballroom full of dancers.`
42. **Jib down reveal, camera cranes down to uncover the subject.**
    - Example: `Jib down reveal, camera cranes down to uncover the subject, a hidden temple doorway beneath jungle canopy.`
